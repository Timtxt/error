• Emotion Grid – Visualization of emotions through animated points, using color, motion, and interaction to represent different emotional states.
  • Voronoi Sketch – A dynamic variation of a Voronoi diagram. User input continuously reshapes the structure, transforming clicks into evolving geometric patterns.
  • Globe Dot Grid – A globe visual made of scalable dots, with adjustable latitude, longitude, and brush size.
  • Pulse Grid – Visualization of heartbeat in six different modes: Sleep, Meditation, Focus, Calm, Sports, and Stress.
  • Star Grid – A scalable point grid arranged in space. As a counterpart to the globe.
  • Calligraphic Lines – Slow movements create thin strokes, while fast gestures generate bold lines. A digital drawing tool that transforms motion into expressive form.
  • Calligraphic Snake – Next generation: this version introduces distinct point animations. Movement becomes visual rhythm—each gesture creates bold, animated lines shaped by speed and direction.
  • Sundial SVG – A playful take on the classic sundial. A digital shadow points the hour, visualizing time through light, angle, and motion.
  • Fade – An illustrative tool that translates user input into gradients made of dots, lines, and squares, turning gestures into layered visual compositions.
  • 64Bit Grid – The canvas is divided into 64-bit, 32-bit, 16-bit, and 8-bit quadrants, showing how bit-depth impacts color and detail.

About

e.r.r.o.r. emerged as part of Tim Petter’s bachelor’s project at HAW Hamburg. The work explores interactive and generative design, using code as a tool for artistic exploration. Through visual systems and user-driven interactions, it pushes the boundaries of traditional graphic design. Scalability, transformation, and real-time responsiveness are central principles, allowing the work to continuously evolve through user input. The website serves as a collection point for these works, making the project accessible and open for free use. errorspace invites users to participate and shape the experience. It offers continuous creative evolution through SVG export options, enabling program-independent further development.
